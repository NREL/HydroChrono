Sphere Model (IEA OES Task 10) - Verification¶

Overview¶

The Sphere Model was initially developed under the IEA OES Task 10 project [NWY+18] to provide a simplified representation of a wave energy converter (WEC) to support the verification of numerical modeling software. The verification results cover a range of testing conditions, including still water decay tests as well as dynamic response analyses in both regular and irregular wave fields. Moreover, some of the Sphere verification results incorporate a damping coefficient to represent a power take-off (PTO).

Model parameters¶

The physical parameters—center of buoyancy, center of gravity, and volume displacement—are crucial for hydrodynamic analysis and are often derived from the geometry and material properties of the sphere.

Results¶

Following the numerical simulation, the time-series data of the heave response is compared for model verification.

References¶

For further information about the sphere model, please refer to [KAT+21] and [WNY+19], which provide comprehensive insights into the methodology and validation undetaken for the sphere model.
